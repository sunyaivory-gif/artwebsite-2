Sunya
art · places

I work with pencil, watercolor, and gouache - trying to catch the colors and small details that make everyday life worth looking at. I'm drawn to things that feel both peaceful and vibrant at the same time.

I admire Claude Monet for how he could make a haystack or a water lily feel monumental through light and color. Camille Pissarro too - his bright palettes somehow never feel forced; they belong exactly where he placed them.

These sketches, paintings, and photographs come from a notebook and a camera carried through different cities and seasons. Some are studied, others are stumbled upon. All of them are attempts to hold onto something worth looking at twice.

"Everything is beautiful, all that matters
is to be able to interpret."
— Camille Pissarro
